On your computer, complete the following steps to create a Dragoon model for Energy Balance.

  1. To start defining a node, click on the Create Node button in the top left corner. The following window should pop up:
  2. The blank dropdown menu labeled Description is for the name and description for the node. Select energy stored in Joe's body each day.
  3. A message should pop up saying "[insert correct message here]". This message is from the tutoring system. It knows what the correct model is and will help you create it. when your blank turns green, it means that you have done this correctly. Click on OK.
  4. Next, select the node Type. Try choosing Parameter.
  5. Oops-- be careful! If you choose the wrong type, the box will turn red.
  6. The correct answer in this case is Accumulator. The problem gives this to you, but often, you will have to read through the prompt and figure it out on your own. See the Key Terms & Concepts at the beginning of this workbook to review node types.
  7. Input the correct initial value. (If you input the incorrect value more than two times, the box will turn yellow with the correct choice.
  8. Enter 345000 for the Initial Value and select calories for units
  9. The next option you should see is Inputs followed by mathematical operations (+,-,*,/). Skip these for now and focus on the Expression box.
  10. The Expression box is where you define the calculation for your node. To do this, you can add Inputs, which are the other nodes in your problem, as well as click on mathematical operations to put them in your calculation. At any point in your node creation, when you think you are done with your expression, press Check Expression to receive feedback on your answer. Above the Expression box, you will see the beginning part of the calculation. In this case, you should see new energy stored = old energy stored +. Finish this calculation with the inputs in the box. In this case, add the input energy balance by clicking on the dropdown option for Inputs and select the correct response. Click Check Expression. The expression box should turn green if you put in the correct calculation.
  11. When you have finished and all the boxes are completed (Inputs will never be completed because it just contains options for the expresion), click Done in the bottom right corner.
  12. Congratulations! You have created your first node. You should immediately notice two things. First, another node has been created in addition to the energy stored one you made. This is because you used it in your calculation in the Description. anything that hasn't been created yet but you use in your calculation should automatically appear when you are done defining your node. Second, you may notice that the node you created, energy stored, has a solid, yellow border around it. The border will change depending on how well you did in creating your node. The border may be solid (signifying the node has been defined completely) or dotted (signifying the node isn't completely defined and you won't be able to graph your model). In addition, the border may be red (you didn't correctly define the node on your own), green (you corretly defined the node on your own, or yellow (the answer was given for you on some nodes). Shoot for making your nodes all green!
  13. Go ahead and double-click the blue energy balance triangle and try to define the node on your own! Hint, the answer is in the prompt.
